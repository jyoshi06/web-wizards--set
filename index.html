Dhanalakshmi Srinivasan University Rules & Regulations

Anti-Ragging Policy

As per DSU's Anti-Ragging Policy (aligned with UGC guidelines):

  • Ragging—including teasing, practical jokes, or actions causing physical/psychological harm—is strictly prohibited.
  • Violators may face legal action: imprisonment up to 2 years and/or fine up to ₹10,000.
  • Convicted students will be dismissed and barred from future admission at any institution.
  • Complaints are investigated immediately; deans have authority to impose punishments like suspension, expulsion, or fines.
  • Awareness programs, orientation sessions, student pledges, helplines, and counseling support are part of the preventive framework.

Examination Rules

  • First 30 minutes: Absentees marked; late arrivals beyond 30 min not allowed entry; no extra time given.
  • No candidate may leave the hall during the first and last 30 minutes.
  • Invigilators must verify that answer booklets include the registration number and other required details; blank pages must be crossed.
  • No communication, mobile phones, or electronic gadgets allowed. Misconduct leads to severe penalties (e.g., cancellation, expulsion).
  • Students must arrive at least half an hour before the exam; no extra time for latecomers.

Hostel Regulations

  • Outings require prior permission (out-pass); students must sign out and sign in.
  • Damage to property results in fines and disciplinary action; cleanliness and proper upkeep are mandatory.
  • Guests are not allowed in student rooms. Misuse of computers or phones can lead to expulsion from hostel.
  • Post-removal from college, hostel membership ceases automatically.
  • Prohibited: Posters on walls, keeping large amounts of cash or valuables in rooms, and vehicles in hostel premises.
  • Lights and fans must be switched off when not in use; roll calls and mess-time alerts are enforced.

Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)

  • Established as per UGC (Prevention of Sexual Harassment) regulations to ensure a safe campus.
  • Comprises faculty, staff, students, and an NGO representative.
  • Punishments for students found guilty may include suspension, expulsion, withholding privileges, or community service.
  • Compensation may be directed to victims based on trauma, medical expenses, and career loss.
  • False or malicious complaints also carry consequences after proper inquiry.
